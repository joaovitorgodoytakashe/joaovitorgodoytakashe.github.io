About

Research at the intersection of animal health and data.

I am a multidisciplinary researcher with a strong background in bioinformatics, molecular biology, and veterinary science, with a focus on livestock health and disease. My experience integrates laboratory techniques, clinical knowledge, and computational analysis to investigate complex biological systems.

I specialize in RNA-Seq data analysis, differential gene expression, and functional enrichment approaches using STAR, DESeq2, R, and Linux-based pipelines. My work centers on transcriptomic analysis in Bos taurus, integrating omics, hematological, metabolic, and clinical traits to better understand immune responses, infectious diseases, and physiological stress.

I am particularly interested in host-pathogen interactions and the molecular mechanisms underlying disease resilience in livestock, with the goal of advancing sustainable and precision animal production systems.
